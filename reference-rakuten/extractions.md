### Presentation slide content (from `meti-kickoff-img_27-rakuten-slide.jpg`)

The visible slide is titled:

> Rakuten AI 2.0を含む既存のTransformerベースのモデルは、AI Agent時代におけるサービスへの提供時に解決すべき重要課題を内在

Translation:

> Existing Transformer-based models, including Rakuten AI 2.0, contain critical issues that must be resolved when providing services in the AI Agent era

The slide lists problems of "既存LLMの抱える課題" (Issues with existing LLMs):
- 高コスト (High cost): Training requires massive GPUs and electricity; inference also requires significant GPU and power
- 長文入力ができない (Cannot handle long text input): e.g., 1 year of email is hundreds of millions of tokens; currently available APIs cannot handle this (as of June 2025, general-purpose API context upper limit is 1M tokens, ~750 pages)
- 推論時は学習しない (Does not learn during inference): Cannot change/update based on output, relying on prompt engineering or user-level conversation-based fine-tuning
- 複雑な環境でのエラー増加 (Increased errors in complex environments): As tasks become more complex and text longer, prompt engineering and RAG alone see increased errors

And "AI Agent適用における潜在的課題" (Potential issues in AI Agent application):
- ユーザー意図の理解 (Understanding user intent): Especially when input text is long or complex
- 複雑なタスクや推論 (Complex tasks and reasoning): Complex day-to-day business tasks and reasoning scenarios

The bottom-left shows an icon labeled "既存Transformer Model (e.g., GPT)" — explicitly framing GPT-style Transformers as the architecture class with these limitations.
